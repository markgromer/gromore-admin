Setting Up Google Ads

Create and configure your Google Ads account so we can pull performance data and help manage your campaigns.

Creating a Google Ads Account
  1. Go to ads.google.com and click "Start Now"
  2. Sign in with your Google account (use the same one you use for Gmail or Google Analytics)
  3. Google will try to walk you through a "Smart Campaign" - you can click "Switch to Expert Mode" at the bottom to skip this
  4. Select "Create an account without a campaign" (bottom of the page)
  5. Confirm your billing country, time zone, and currency, then click "Submit"
Tip: Choose your time zone and currency carefully. These cannot be changed after account creation.
Finding Your Customer ID
  1. Once logged into Google Ads, look at the top-right corner of the page
  2. Your Customer ID is the 10-digit number in the format XXX-XXX-XXXX
  3. Copy this number and paste it into your Connections page
Your Customer ID is how we connect to your specific Google Ads account. Without it, we cannot pull your ad performance data.
Setting Up Billing
  1. Click the tools icon (wrench) in the top menu, then "Billing" > "Settings"
  2. Add a payment method (credit card, debit card, or bank account)
  3. Google Ads charges you based on actual ad spend, not a flat fee
Important: Your ads will not run until billing is set up, even if campaigns are created and enabled.
Connecting Google Ads to This Platform
  1. Go to your Connections page
  2. Click "Connect Google Account" and sign in with the Google account that owns your Ads account
  3. Grant the requested permissions (read your ad data, manage campaigns)
  4. Enter your Customer ID in the "Google Ads Customer ID" field and save

Setting Up Facebook Ads

Create a Meta Business Suite and ad account to run Facebook and Instagram ads.

Creating a Meta Business Account
  1. Go to business.facebook.com
  2. Click "Create Account" and log in with your personal Facebook account
  3. Enter your business name, your name, and your business email
  4. Fill in your business details (address, phone, website) and click Submit
Tip: Your personal Facebook profile is used for login only. Your personal posts and friends are not visible to your business account.
Creating an Ad Account
  1. In Meta Business Suite, click the gear icon (Settings)
  2. Under "Accounts", click "Ad Accounts" then "Add" > "Create a new ad account"
  3. Name your ad account (e.g., "Main Ad Account"), set time zone and currency
  4. Add a payment method under "Payment Settings"
Finding Your Ad Account ID
  1. In Business Settings, go to "Ad Accounts" in the sidebar
  2. Click on your ad account name
  3. Your Ad Account ID is shown at the top - it is a number like act_123456789
Installing the Facebook Pixel
  1. In Meta Business Suite, go to "Events Manager"
  2. Click "Connect Data Sources" > "Web" > "Facebook Pixel"
  3. Name your pixel and enter your website URL
  4. Choose your installation method:
    • Partner Integration (easiest) - works with WordPress, Shopify, Wix, Squarespace
    • Manual Install - copy the pixel code snippet and paste it into your website's <head> tag
  5. Use the Meta Pixel Helper Chrome extension to verify it is firing correctly
Important: Without the pixel installed, Facebook cannot track conversions on your website, and your campaigns will not optimize properly.
Connecting Facebook to This Platform
  1. Go to your Connections page
  2. Click "Connect Meta Account" and sign in with Facebook
  3. Select the ad account you want to connect and confirm permissions

Google Analytics (GA4)

Track who visits your website, where they come from, and what they do once they land.

Creating a GA4 Property
  1. Go to analytics.google.com
  2. Click "Admin" (gear icon) in the bottom-left
  3. Click "Create" > "Property"
  4. Enter your website name, select your reporting time zone and currency
  5. Choose your industry and business size, then click "Create"
Adding the Tracking Code
  1. After creating your property, you will see "Data Streams" - click "Web"
  2. Enter your website URL and stream name, then click "Create Stream"
  3. You will see a "Measurement ID" (format: G-XXXXXXXXXX) - copy this
  4. Install the tracking code:
    • Google Tag Manager (recommended) - create a new "Google Analytics: GA4 Configuration" tag
    • Direct install - copy the gtag.js snippet and paste it into every page's <head> tag
    • WordPress - use a plugin like "Site Kit by Google" or paste the code in your theme's header
Finding Your GA4 Property ID
  1. In Google Analytics, click "Admin" (gear icon)
  2. Under "Property", look at the top - your Property ID is a number like 123456789
  3. This is different from the Measurement ID (G-XXXXXXXX). We need the numeric Property ID.
When you connect your Google account on the Connections page, we automatically detect your GA4 properties. You just need to make sure the tracking code is installed on your website.

Google Search Console

See how your website appears in Google search results: what keywords you rank for, how many clicks you get, and where you can improve.

Adding Your Website
  1. Go to search.google.com/search-console
  2. Click "Add property"
  3. Choose "URL prefix" and enter your full website URL (e.g., https://yourdomain.com)
  4. Verify ownership using one of these methods:
    • HTML tag - add a meta tag to your homepage's <head>
    • Google Analytics - automatic if GA4 is installed (easiest)
    • DNS record - add a TXT record at your domain registrar
Tip: If you already have GA4 installed, Search Console verification is usually automatic. Just use the same Google account.
What Data Shows Up

Search Console data takes a few days to start appearing after verification. It shows:

  • Search queries - the actual words people type to find you
  • Click-through rate - how often people click your result vs. just seeing it
  • Average position - where you rank on average for each keyword
  • Indexed pages - which of your pages Google knows about

Conversion Tracking

Track the actions that matter: form submissions, phone calls, purchases, bookings. Without conversion tracking, you're flying blind.

Google Ads Conversions
  1. In Google Ads, click "Goals" > "Conversions" > "Summary"
  2. Click the blue "+" to create a new conversion action
  3. Choose "Website" for tracking form fills or purchases
  4. Set up the conversion:
    • Category - "Submit lead form", "Purchase", "Book appointment", etc.
    • Value - assign a dollar value if you know what a lead is worth (helps with ROAS reporting)
    • Count - "One" for leads (one per person), "Every" for purchases
  5. Install using Google Tag Manager or the global site tag
Facebook/Meta Conversions
  1. In Events Manager, click your pixel
  2. Click "Add Events" > "From the Pixel" (or use the Event Setup Tool)
  3. Add standard events:
    • Lead - fires when someone submits a form
    • Purchase - fires on your order confirmation page
    • Contact - fires when someone clicks to call or email
GA4 Conversions
  1. In GA4, go to "Admin" > "Events"
  2. Find the event you want (e.g., form_submit, purchase) or create a custom event
  3. Toggle "Mark as conversion" next to the event name
Why this matters: If conversions are not tracked, our reports will show clicks and spend but cannot calculate cost-per-lead, ROAS, or whether your campaigns are actually working.

Call Tracking

If you get leads by phone, call tracking lets you measure which ads and keywords drive calls.

Why Use Call Tracking
  • Know exactly which ad or keyword drove each phone call
  • Get accurate lead counts in your reports instead of guessing
  • Record calls for quality assurance and training
  • Track missed calls so you can follow up
Popular Call Tracking Services
  • CallRail - popular for service businesses, integrates with Google Ads and GA4
  • CallTrackingMetrics - enterprise-level features, multi-channel attribution
  • Google Ads call extensions - free built-in option, tracks calls from ads directly
Setting Up Google Ads Call Extensions
  1. In Google Ads, go to "Ads & assets" > "Assets"
  2. Click the blue "+" > "Call"
  3. Enter your business phone number
  4. Enable "Call reporting" to track calls as conversions
  5. Set a minimum call duration for a conversion (e.g., 60 seconds) so short wrong-number calls don't count
Enter your call tracking number on the My Business page so our AI includes it in generated ad copy.

OpenAI API Key

Use your own OpenAI API key to power the AI features: Ad Builder, Action Plans, report generation, and more. This gives you control over which model to use and lets costs go directly to your OpenAI billing.

Getting Your API Key
  1. Go to platform.openai.com and create an account (or log in)
  2. Click your profile icon (top-right) > "API Keys" or go directly to platform.openai.com/api-keys
  3. Click "Create new secret key", give it a name (e.g., "GroMore Dashboard"), and click "Create"
  4. Copy the key immediately - it starts with sk- and is only shown once
  5. Paste it into the AI Configuration section on your Connections page
Copy it now: OpenAI only shows the full key once. If you lose it, you will need to create a new one.
Adding Billing to OpenAI
  1. Go to platform.openai.com/account/billing
  2. Click "Add payment method" and enter a credit card
  3. Add at least $10 in credits - this is enough to run the platform for weeks. Most users spend $2-5/month total.
  4. Set a monthly usage limit to cap costs (e.g., $20/month is plenty for even heavy usage)
Cost breakdown: A single Ad Builder generation costs about $0.01-0.03. A full campaign plan with proofreading costs $0.02-0.05. A monthly report analysis costs $0.01-0.02. Even generating ads every day, you are unlikely to spend more than $5/month. The $10 starting credit gives you plenty of room.
Choosing a Model

When you configure your key on the Connections page, you can also pick your AI model:

  • GPT-4o Mini - fast and affordable, great for most tasks. This is the default.
  • GPT-4o - more capable, slightly higher cost. Good if you want more nuanced ad copy.
  • GPT-4 Turbo - powerful model, handles long reports well.
  • GPT-4.1 / GPT-4.1 Mini - latest generation models. Best quality and speed.
  • o3-mini / o4-mini - reasoning models that "think" before answering. Best for complex analysis.

Google Maps API

Power the Local Rank Heatmap and competitor intelligence features. See exactly where your business shows up (and where it doesn't) across your entire service area.

What This Connection Does
  • Local Rank Heatmap: Scans a grid of points across your service area and checks where your business ranks in Google Maps results for your target keywords. You get a visual heatmap showing green (top 3), yellow (page 1), and red (not ranking) for every point.
  • Competitor Intel: See which competitors show up at each grid point. Identify which areas they dominate and where you have opportunity.
  • GMB Monitoring: Track your Google Business Profile visibility over time. Spot ranking drops before they cost you leads.
Getting a Google Maps API Key
  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console and sign in with your Google account
  2. Create a new project (or select an existing one). Name it something like "GroMore Maps"
  3. Go to "APIs & Services" > "Library" in the left sidebar
  4. Search for and enable these APIs:
    • Places API (New) - required for current place search and business lookup requests
    • Places API - required as the compatibility fallback for place lookups
    • Geocoding API - required to turn searched addresses, cities, and scan centers into map coordinates
    • Maps JavaScript API - required for rendering the heatmap and interactive map views
  5. Go to "APIs & Services" > "Credentials"
  6. Click "Create Credentials" > "API Key"
  7. Copy the key and paste it into the Google Maps API Key field on your Connections page
Setting Up Billing for Google Cloud
  1. In the Cloud Console, go to "Billing" in the left sidebar
  2. Link a billing account to your project
  3. Google gives you $200/month in free Maps API usage - this covers thousands of heatmap scans. Most users never exceed the free tier.
Cost note: A single heatmap scan with a 7x7 grid uses about 50 Places API calls, costing roughly $0.80. With Google's $200 free monthly credit, you can run around 250 scans per month at zero cost.
Linking Your Google Business Profile
  1. On the Connections page, look for the "Google Place ID" field
  2. Use the search tool to find your business by name
  3. Select your listing from the results - this links the heatmap scans to your specific business
Important: Without a Place ID, heatmap scans cannot identify your business in the results. Make sure you have a claimed and verified Google Business Profile at business.google.com first.
Restricting Your API Key (Recommended)

For security, restrict your API key so it can only be used by your dashboard:

  1. In the Cloud Console, go to "Credentials" and click on your API key
  2. Under "API restrictions," select "Restrict key" and check only Places API (New), Places API, Geocoding API, and Maps JavaScript API
  3. Click "Save"
Tip: After saving the key in GroMore, use Test Google Maps Key on the Connections page. The test checks Places API (New), Places API, and Geocoding so you can see exactly which service still needs to be enabled.

OpenPhone / Quo SMS

Connect Quo, formerly OpenPhone, so Warren can receive inbound SMS, create or update lead threads, and send outbound texts from your configured business number.

What This Connection Does
  • Turns SMS into a Warren lead channel instead of keeping those conversations trapped in a separate app.
  • Lets inbound text replies create or update lead threads in the inbox.
  • Lets Warren send outbound texts from your configured Quo number.
  • Gives you connection testing, phone-number lookup, and test-SMS tools on the Connections page.
What You Need
  • A Quo / OpenPhone account with API access.
  • Your Quo API key.
  • Your sending number in E.164 format, for example +15551234567.
  • Access to your webhook settings in Quo / OpenPhone.
Step 1: Save The API Key And Number
  1. Go to your Connections page.
  2. Find the Warren setup section.
  3. Paste your OpenPhone / Quo API Key.
  4. Enter your OpenPhone / Quo Number in E.164 format.
  5. Save the settings.
Important: the number should be a full SMS-capable business number in the format +1XXXXXXXXXX. If the number format is wrong, sends and matching can fail even if the API key is valid.
Step 2: Add The Inbound Webhook
  1. After saving your key, stay on the Warren section of the Connections page.
  2. Copy the Inbound SMS Webhook URL shown there.
  3. In Quo / OpenPhone, open Settings > Webhooks.
  4. Add a webhook using the copied URL.
  5. Select the message.received event so incoming SMS posts back to Warren.
What Warren expects: inbound SMS is received through the brand-specific webhook URL and processed as a message.received event. That is what creates or updates the right lead thread in the inbox.
Step 3: Optional Webhook Secret

If you want signature verification turned on, copy your webhook secret from Quo / OpenPhone and save it in the OpenPhone Webhook Secret field on the Connections page.

  • If a secret is saved, Warren verifies signed webhook requests before accepting them.
  • If you leave the field blank, the webhook can still work, but you lose that validation layer.
Step 4: Test It Before You Rely On It
  1. Use Test Connection on the Connections page to confirm Warren can reach the Quo account and list available phone numbers.
  2. Use Send Test SMS to text your own phone.
  3. Reply to that SMS from your phone and confirm the reply appears in Warren.
Most common setup problems: wrong number format, webhook not set to message.received, or a missing webhook secret when signed requests are expected.

Once connected, SMS can feed directly into Warren's lead qualification, quoting, and follow-up flow instead of forcing your team to bounce between tools.

WordPress Connection

Publish AI-generated blog posts directly to your WordPress website. No copy-pasting needed.

What This Connection Does
  • The AI writes SEO-optimized blog content based on your business, services, and target audience
  • Posts get pushed directly to your WordPress site as drafts (or published, your choice)
  • Saves time on content marketing - go from idea to published post in minutes
Requirements
  • A self-hosted WordPress site (wordpress.org, not wordpress.com free tier)
  • WordPress REST API must be enabled (it is by default on most hosts)
  • An Application Password (built into WordPress 5.6+)
  • The GroMore Publisher helper plugin only if your host blocks outside publish requests before WordPress can process them
Creating an Application Password
  1. Log into your WordPress admin dashboard
  2. Go to Users > Profile (or click "Edit" on your user)
  3. Scroll down to the "Application Passwords" section
  4. Enter a name (e.g., "GroMore") and click "Add New Application Password"
  5. Copy the generated password immediately - it is only shown once. It will look like xxxx xxxx xxxx xxxx xxxx xxxx (with spaces)
Connecting
  1. Go to your Connections page
  2. Enter your WordPress Site URL (e.g., https://yourdomain.com)
  3. Enter your WordPress Username (the one you log in with)
  4. Enter the Application Password you just created
  5. Save - the platform will test the connection
Not seeing "Application Passwords"? Some security plugins (like Wordfence or iThemes) can disable this feature. Check your security plugin settings, or ask your web host for help enabling it.
When Direct Publishing Is Blocked

Most WordPress sites publish directly with the Site URL, username, and Application Password above. Some hosts return a CAPTCHA or firewall page before WordPress sees the request. On those sites, install or update the GroMore Publisher helper plugin once, then configure it inside WordPress under Settings > GroMore Publisher.

Download helper plugin zip Open copy-paste PHP

Option A: Upload the plugin zip
  1. Click Download helper plugin zip above
  2. In WordPress, go to Plugins > Add New > Upload Plugin
  3. Upload the downloaded zip, install it, and activate it
  4. Go to Settings > GroMore Publisher
Option B: Paste the PHP manually
  1. Click Open copy-paste PHP above and copy the full PHP file contents
  2. In your WordPress files, create this folder: wp-content/plugins/gromore-warren-publisher
  3. Create this file: wp-content/plugins/gromore-warren-publisher/gromore-warren-publisher.php
  4. Paste the copied PHP into that file and save it
  5. In WordPress, go to Plugins and activate GroMore Warren Publisher Endpoint
  6. Go to Settings > GroMore Publisher
Helper plugin settings
  1. Confirm the prefilled GroMore App URL and Brand ID in the plugin settings
  2. Enter the same WordPress username and Application Password used on the GroMore Connections page
  3. Save settings, then click Pull Queued Post Now to publish any queued posts
How this helps: The helper pulls queued posts from GroMore from inside WordPress, so the host firewall no longer has to allow GroMore to push into the site. It also checks automatically every 5 minutes through WP-Cron, but WP-Cron only runs when WordPress receives traffic. Use Pull Queued Post Now for immediate publishing. Sites where direct publishing already works do not need the helper.

Reading Your Reports

Understand what every section of your monthly report means and what to do about it.

The Health Grade

Your overall grade (A through F) is based on how your actual results compare to your targets. An "A" means you are hitting or exceeding all KPIs. A "C" means things are running but there is room for improvement.

KPI Cards

These show your key numbers with color coding:

  • Green - on track or ahead of target
  • Red - behind target, needs attention

Set your targets (CPA, leads, ROAS) on the My Business page so these comparisons are meaningful.

What's Going Well / Needs Attention

AI-generated highlights that call out the most important wins and concerns for the month. These are based on actual data changes, not generic advice.

Channel Metrics

Each ad platform (Google Ads, Meta Ads) gets its own section showing spend, impressions, clicks, CTR, conversions, and cost per conversion. Compare these side-by-side to see which platform performs better for your business.

Action Plan

Prioritized recommendations based on your data. Each action includes why it matters and step-by-step instructions. Actions labeled "High" priority should be done first.

Using the Ad Builder

Generate ad copy and campaign ideas powered by your actual performance data.

How It Works
  1. Go to Ad Builder
  2. Select the platform (Google Search, Facebook/Instagram) and your objective (leads, traffic, awareness)
  3. Add any specific notes or context (seasonal offers, new services, etc.)
  4. Click "Generate" and the AI will create ad copy based on your brand profile, current offers, and performance data

Budget Management

How to set, adjust, and think about your advertising budget.

Setting Daily Budgets

Both Google Ads and Facebook use daily budgets. Your monthly spend will be approximately: daily budget x 30.4 days.

  • Google Ads - set at the campaign level. Google may spend up to 2x your daily budget on high-traffic days, but will not exceed your monthly cap (daily x 30.4)
  • Facebook Ads - can set daily or lifetime budgets. Daily budgets are easier to manage for ongoing campaigns
Budget Changes from the Dashboard
  1. Go to Campaigns
  2. Click on any campaign to see its details
  3. Use the budget adjustment section to increase or decrease spend
Heads up: Budget changes take effect within a few hours. Avoid making big changes frequently - give campaigns at least 3-5 days to adjust after a budget change before evaluating results.
How Much Should You Spend?

There is no universal answer, but here are some guidelines:

  • Start with what you are comfortable losing during the learning phase (usually 2-4 weeks)
  • If your target CPA is $50 and you want 10 leads/month, you need at least $500/month budget
  • Split between platforms based on where your audience is: B2B companies often do better on Google, while B2C/local services can thrive on Facebook
  • Watch your cost-per-lead in your reports - if it is below your target, consider increasing budget to scale up

Glossary

Common advertising and analytics terms you will see in your reports and dashboard.

  • Impressions - how many times your ad was shown
  • Clicks - how many times someone clicked your ad
  • CTR (Click-Through Rate) - clicks divided by impressions, expressed as a percentage. Higher is better.
  • CPC (Cost Per Click) - how much you paid on average for each click
  • CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) - how much you paid on average for each lead or conversion
  • CPL (Cost Per Lead) - same as CPA but specifically for lead generation
  • Conversions - completed actions: form fills, phone calls, purchases, bookings
  • Conversion Rate - percentage of clicks that result in a conversion
  • ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) - revenue divided by ad spend. A ROAS of 5x means $5 revenue for every $1 spent.
  • Quality Score - Google's 1-10 rating of your ad and landing page relevance. Higher scores mean lower costs.
  • Search Terms - the actual words someone typed before clicking your ad (can differ from your keywords)
  • Negative Keywords - words you add to prevent your ads from showing for irrelevant searches
  • Pixel - a small piece of code on your website that tracks visitor behavior for Facebook/Meta
  • Audience - the group of people your ads are targeted to, based on demographics, interests, or behavior
  • Retargeting - showing ads to people who previously visited your website
  • Landing Page - the page someone arrives at after clicking your ad
