# W.A.R.R.E.N. Website Brief

Date: April 12, 2026

Purpose: This document is the handoff brief for a website design and development team. It is intended to help them build a high-conversion marketing website for W.A.R.R.E.N. without forcing them to reverse-engineer the product from internal product docs.

This is not the full feature inventory. The full reference list lives in `GROMORE_FEATURES.md` and should be treated as appendix material, not the main source for page structure or messaging.

## 1. What This Website Must Do

The website is meant to:

- Position W.A.R.R.E.N. as the hero product
- Make GroMore feel like the company behind it, not the headline
- Create excitement and momentum around the product
- Explain the product clearly enough that the right buyers understand what it does
- Convert visitors into demo requests, beta applications, or direct conversations
- Make the platform feel real, modern, powerful, and operationally useful

This should not feel like a generic SaaS website. It should feel like a serious growth operating system for home service businesses and the teams that run them.

## 2. Brand Positioning

### Primary Brand

W.A.R.R.E.N.

Expanded as:

Weighted Analysis for Revenue, Reach, Engagement & Navigation

### Supporting Brand

GroMore Media

GroMore is the company and operator behind W.A.R.R.E.N. The website should make that clear, but W.A.R.R.E.N. should be the dominant product identity.

### Positioning Statement

W.A.R.R.E.N. is an AI growth operator for home service businesses that connects lead handling, campaign execution, reporting, recommendations, and next actions in one system.

### Category Statement

This is not just a dashboard.

This is not just an AI chatbot.

This is not just campaign software.

It is a growth operating system built to help service businesses respond faster, launch smarter, see what is working, and know what to do next.

## 3. Ideal Customer

### Primary Audience

Home service business owners and operators.

Examples:

- Plumbing
- HVAC
- Roofing
- Electrical
- Cleaning
- Landscaping
- Pest control
- Similar local service businesses

### Secondary Audience

Agencies or operators managing growth for local service businesses.

### Buyer Problems

The website should be built around these problems:

- Leads are coming in, but follow-up is inconsistent or slow
- Marketing reporting exists, but it does not turn into action
- Campaign launches and changes take too much manual coordination
- Teams are using too many disconnected tools
- Owners do not know what is working, what is broken, or what should happen next
- Agencies and internal teams lose time moving between reporting, ad ops, lead response, CRM work, and strategy

## 4. Core Promise

### Before W.A.R.R.E.N.

- Leads sit too long
- Reporting is passive
- Campaign changes are fragmented
- Teams operate from disconnected systems
- Owners feel uncertain and reactive

### After W.A.R.R.E.N.

- Leads get handled faster
- Performance is interpreted in plain English
- Campaigns can move from plan to launch inside one workflow
- Recommendations are connected to execution
- Teams know what happened, what matters, and what to do next

### Transformation Statement

W.A.R.R.E.N. turns growth operations from scattered and reactive into coordinated and visible.

## 5. Website Goal

The site needs one dominant conversion goal.

### Primary CTA

Book a demo

### Secondary CTA

Apply for beta or join waitlist

### Tertiary CTA

Talk to the team

Do not treat all CTAs equally in the design. The site should be structured primarily around demo conversion.

## 6. Messaging Priorities

The site should emphasize these ideas first:

1. W.A.R.R.E.N. helps service businesses respond, report, launch, and improve from one system.
2. It combines AI assistance with operator control, not reckless automation.
3. It is built for real business outcomes, not novelty.
4. It is designed for home service businesses and the teams that support them.
5. It closes the loop between insight and action.

## 7. Messaging Guardrails

Use language like:

- AI growth operator
- AI-powered
- Operator-in-the-loop
- Guided execution
- Controlled launch workflow
- Recommendations tied to action
- Unified system
- Faster follow-up
- Clearer decisions
- Better visibility

Avoid language like:

- Fully autonomous
- Set it and forget it
- AI runs your business for you
- Completely hands-free ads
- Automatic growth with no oversight

If explaining the product, the correct posture is:

W.A.R.R.E.N. helps teams move faster and make better decisions. It can assist with response, planning, launch, analysis, and recommendations, but the product should not be marketed as an uncontrolled black-box automation engine.

## 8. Product Pillars To Feature On The Website

Do not flatten the site into a giant feature dump. These are the major pillars that should drive the information architecture.

### Pillar 1: AI Lead Response and Follow-Up

- SMS conversations
- Facebook Messenger
- Meta lead forms
- Confidence gating
- Hand-off rules
- Follow-up automation
- Pipeline movement

### Pillar 2: Unified Lead Inbox and Pipeline

- One inbox across channels
- Thread visibility
- Pipeline stage management
- Warren draft replies
- Human takeover when needed

### Pillar 3: Performance Dashboard and Recommendations

- Health scoring
- Plain-English reporting
- KPI pacing
- W.A.R.R.E.N.'s call
- Concerns and highlights

### Pillar 4: Campaign Planning and Execution

- Campaign generation
- Quick-launch workflow
- Strategy selection
- Drafts and preflight checks
- Pause, resume, budget changes, negative keywords

### Pillar 5: AI Assistant and Specialist Team

- W.A.R.R.E.N. chat assistant
- Specialist AI agents
- Findings and recommendations
- Missions and next-best actions

### Pillar 6: Business Tools Around Growth

- Google Business Profile audit
- Competitor intelligence
- Heatmaps
- Blog tools
- Post scheduler
- Creative tools
- Commercial account engine for service businesses selling to apartments, HOAs, and property managers should be treated as a supporting product page or expansion page, not a homepage-first pillar

### Pillar 7: Flexible Support Layer

- VA Desk is planned as a flexible support add-on
- Token-based fulfillment model
- Internal support pool by specialty
- Position this as an upcoming support layer, not a core launch promise if the implementation is not live yet

## 9. Features That Matter Most For Homepage Placement

These deserve top-level treatment on the homepage:

- AI lead response and follow-up
- Unified inbox and pipeline
- Performance reporting with recommendations
- Campaign launch and optimization workflow
- AI assistant and guided next actions

These belong lower on the page or on product detail pages:

- Blog tools
- Post scheduler
- Hiring hub
- Commercial account workflows
- Drive integration
- Creative canvas details
- Deep admin features
- Internal diagnostic tools

## 10. Trust and Proof Requirements

The site should include trust elements that make the product feel real.

### Must Include

- Real product screenshots or high-fidelity product mockups
- Clear explanation of who the product is for
- Specific use cases, not vague AI language
- Proof that this is a serious operational system

### Strongly Recommended

- Screens from the lead inbox
- Screens from the dashboard
- Screens from campaign workflow
- Screens from W.A.R.R.E.N. assistant
- Clear explanation of supported channels and integrations

### Social Proof Ideas

- Testimonials from early users
- Beta case study snapshots
- Industry categories served
- Metrics or business outcomes if supportable

Do not invent claims or overstate results.

## 11. Visual Direction

The website should feel:

- Bold
- Premium
- Operator-grade
- Fast
- High-conviction
- Modern
- Slightly aggressive in energy, but credible

It should not feel:

- Soft generic SaaS
- Empty enterprise bland
- Purple-on-white AI template work
- Overcrowded with equal-weight feature boxes

### Design Mood

- Dark, rich, high-contrast sections are acceptable if handled intentionally
- Strong typography
- Product-led visuals
- Motion used with purpose
- Conversion-focused layout with clear information hierarchy

### Important UI Note

This is not a brochure site for a traditional agency. It should feel like a product company with a real operating system.

## 13. Homepage Structure Recommendation

Suggested order:

1. Hero
2. Trust / credibility strip
3. What W.A.R.R.E.N. actually does
4. Core product pillars
5. Product screenshots or workflow sequence
6. Why this is different from other tools
7. Use cases by business type or operator type
8. Testimonials / proof
9. FAQ / objections
10. Final CTA

## 14. Hero Requirements

The hero should communicate:

- What the product is
- Who it is for
- Why it matters
- What action to take next

### Hero Job To Be Done

Within 5 seconds, a visitor should understand:

- This is for business growth and operations
- This is productized, not vague consulting
- It handles both lead flow and marketing workflow
- It is built for local/home service businesses

### Hero Content Direction

Possible angle:

Run lead response, campaign execution, reporting, and next actions from one AI-powered operator.

Subtext direction:

W.A.R.R.E.N. helps home service businesses and growth teams move faster, follow up better, see what is working, and take action from one system.

## 15. Objections The Site Must Answer

The site should answer these objections directly:

- Is this just another AI wrapper?
- Is this only for agencies or also for business owners?
- Does it actually do anything beyond reporting?
- Is it dangerous, uncontrolled automation?
- Will this replace my team or support my team?
- How much setup is required?
- What channels and platforms does it connect to?
- Can I still stay in control?

## 16. Integrations To Show

These can be shown as ecosystem proof:

- Google Ads
- Google Analytics 4
- Google Search Console
- Google Business Profile
- Meta Ads
- Facebook Messenger
- Meta Lead Forms
- WordPress
- Stripe
- CRM integrations such as GoHighLevel, HubSpot, Sweep & Go, Jobber, or webhook-based systems

Do not clutter the site with every implementation detail. Treat integrations as trust and compatibility signals.

## 17. Conversion Requirements

The design team should optimize for:

- Clear CTA visibility above the fold
- Repeated CTA moments throughout the page
- Fast understanding of the product category
- Excitement without confusion
- Low friction demo request path

### Required Conversion Elements

- Primary hero CTA
- CTA after core pillars
- CTA near proof section
- Strong closing CTA at page bottom

## 18. Technical Constraints and Deployment Notes

- The marketing website and the app should be treated as separate deployed experiences
- The website should drive users into demo or application flow, not attempt to function as the app itself
- The product app should remain on its own deployment and likely its own subdomain
- The website must be responsive and mobile-ready
- SEO should be treated as important
- Website copy should be indexable, clear, and structured for future growth pages
